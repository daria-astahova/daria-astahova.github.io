Overview

The Idaho History Project is an interactive exhibit designed to immerse visitors in the rich history of Idaho, while educating them about locations that are often difficult to access or rarely visited. During the development of the project, the team selected Swan Falls Dam as the primary focus of the exhibit. Swan Falls Dam is a hydroelectric dam located on the Snake River in southwestern Idaho and holds significant importance in the region’s history and infrastructure. As one of the oldest hydroelectric dams in Idaho, it has played a critical role in providing power to surrounding communities while also becoming a well-known recreational destination. Beyond its historical value, Swan Falls is known for its dramatic geological history, stunning views of the Snake River, and its location within the Morley Nelson Snake River Birds of Prey National Conservation Area, making it an important site for both geological research and wildlife conservation.

Challenges & Solutions

One of the main challenges we faced during the development process was the connection and seamless synchronization between Unreal Engine and the thee computer. This integration was crucial for achieving the desired interactive experience, but it required careful configuration and troubleshooting to ensure smooth communication between the two platforms. Using nDisplay to address this challenge, we conducted testing and researched to resolve any issues that arose during development.
